My Journey to Enugu

N.Y.S.C means National Youth Service Corps.
It is a scheme that trains and educate Nigerian
youths on the need for patriotism, unity and
youth development within the country.
It is a compulsory scheme for graduates across the country.

My NYSC journey started at Agwu Camp
in Enugu in February, 2014.
The 3-weeks Boot Camp was really
a training ground with the various
parade by different Cadet groups.
The camp Commanders leaves no stone unturned
in ensuring that we were drilled to
the core early in the morning (by 5a.m)
under the rain and in the sunshine.

We participated in other activities like

  • mountain climbing
  • climbing of ropes
  • slipping through types
  • falling like a mango tree

The Camp exposed me to people from diverse cultures
and tribal nationalities. I met other graduates
from all walks of life from diverse
tribes, cultures and background. Also, I learnt
to cook delicacies from other tribes and made some new friends. Their names:

  1. Mariam Idahosa
  2. Dayo Wilson
  3. Eunice Okocha
  4. Abiodun Martins
  5. Dr. Wura Lawson
  6. Charity Ayoola

For my PPA, i was posted to Nsukka Town.
I served as a teacher in
Obimo Community Secondary School, Nsukka,
where i taught the students in the
Junior class Business Studies, Social Studies and Commerce.

I really learnt a lot and enjoyed one
mandatory service year while it lasted.
My service year ended on a successful note
in February 2015 with a Passing-Out-Ceremony and award of Certificate
to all successful participants.
